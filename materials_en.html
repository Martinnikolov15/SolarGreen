Materials

Using solar energy requires recycling the solar panels.

We take back the materials they were built from and put them back into making new ones.

Glass
The protective glass of a solar panel makes up about 70% of its weight. Through our advanced recycling technology, we recover high-purity glass, suitable for container and, in the future, for solar glass.
Plastics
Various plastics are used in solar panels, the most common being EVA and PVF. They make up about 9% of the total mass. Despite their difficulty in recycling, we are looking for new applications, away from incineration.
Aluminium
The aluminum in solar panels is mainly in the frame and makes up about 15% of the mass. The recovered aluminum is passed on to large foundries, saving about 95% of the energy compared to newly mined aluminum.
Copper
The busbars and connecting cables of the solar panel contain 1% copper. It can be fully recycled and used for new cables or wires.
Silver
Silver makes up only 0.1% of the mass of a solar panel, but it is its most valuable material. It is found on the front and back of the cells and serves as an electrical conductor. After recycling, it is purified and reused for technical applications.
Silicon
Silicon is the technical heart of the solar panel. It converts solar energy into electricity through the photovoltaic effect. The recovered silicon is processed into pure silicon by our partner.
